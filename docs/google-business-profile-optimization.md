## 6. Review engine — your #1 priority

You have the rating; you need the volume and a steady drip. Target: **2–4 new reviews a month**, ongoing.

**Get your review short-link:** in the GBP dashboard → "Ask for reviews" → copy the `g.page/r/...` link. Use it everywhere below.

**Request message (text/email after a project ships):**
```
Hi [Name] — really enjoyed building [project] with you. If you've got 30 seconds,
a quick Google review makes a big difference to a small studio like ours.
Here's the direct link: [review link]. Thank you! — Charlie
```

**Tip:** ask the client to mention *what you did and where* ("website for our Exeter salon") — keyword-rich reviews help you rank. Don't script it, just nudge.

**Reply to every review** (Google rewards owner responses). Templates:

- _5-star, brief:_ "Thanks [Name] — it was a pleasure building your site. Glad you're happy with it, and we're here whenever you need us. — Charlie"
- _5-star, detailed:_ "Really appreciate this, [Name]. Loved working on [project] with you — getting it fast and ranking locally was the goal, so it's great to hear it's landing. Thanks for trusting Charged Studio. — Charlie"
- _Mentions a service:_ "Thank you [Name]! [Branding/the redesign] was a fun one. Wishing [business] every success — shout if you ever want to add [SEO/a care plan]. — Charlie"

## 7. Google Posts — publish weekly

Posts expire (~7 days of prominence), so this is a cadence, not a one-off. Start with these five:

1. **Offer post — Free Website Audit**
   > "Free website audit. We'll show you honestly whether your site is building trust and generating leads — or quietly holding your business back. No pitch, no call you didn't ask for. Request yours →" (link to /audit)

2. **Update — what you do**
   > "We build fast, custom websites for service businesses across Exeter and Devon — built to rank locally and turn visitors into enquiries. Not slow templates." (link to /services/websites)

3. **Showcase — Devon Nurseries case study**
   > "How we built a fast, lead-generating website for Devon Nurseries. See the work →" (link to /devon-nurseries)

4. **Update — the USP**
   > "Every Charged Studio build is run by an APM-qualified project manager. That means a clear plan, real timelines, and a site delivered on time. Built to perform, charged to last."

5. **Update — care plans / retainers (soft)**
   > "We don't just build sites and disappear. Our care plans keep your website fast, secure and up to date — so you can get on with running your business."

After these, post roughly weekly: a new project, a tip, a before/after, an offer.

## 9. Quick wins to switch on

- **Messaging** — turn it on (reply fast; slow replies get auto-disabled).
- **Booking link** — add your Calendly: `https://calendly.com/hello-chargedstudio/15min`.
- **Website link** — point it at the homepage; add a UTM (`?utm_source=google&utm_medium=gbp&utm_campaign=profile`) so GA4 shows GBP traffic.
- **Opening date** — set the date the business started, so the profile can show "X years in business" like competitors.
- **Hours** — confirm they're accurate; set holiday hours so you never show "may be closed".
- **NAP consistency** — make sure Name / phone match *exactly* across the website footer, GBP, LinkedIn, Instagram and any directories. Inconsistency hurts local ranking.
