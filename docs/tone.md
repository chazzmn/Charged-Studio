# Tone of Voice — Charged Studio

> Every word on the site should sound like it was written by someone who is confident, direct, and deeply invested in the client's success. Not corporate. Not casual. Sharp.

---

## The Voice in One Line

**Confident, warm, and direct. We know what we're doing and we care about your business.**

---

## What Charged Studio Actually Does (positioning — inform all copy)

**Primary:** Websites — the core product. Custom, modern, high-performance.  
**Secondary:** Software & apps — bespoke digital products for growing businesses.  
**Supporting:** SEO & digital presence — getting found and staying visible.  
**Ongoing:** Retainers & partnerships — long-term relationships, not one-off projects.  
**Side:** Branding — available, but not the lead offer.

Copy should lead with websites and digital products. Branding is a supporting service, not a headline.

---

## Personality Traits

| Trait | What it means in practice |
|---|---|
| **Confident** | We don't hedge. "We build" not "We can help you build". |
| **Direct** | Short sentences. No waffle. Get to the point. |
| **Premium** | Precise word choice. Nothing sloppy or generic. |
| **Human** | Not robotic. Not corporate. A real person wrote this. |
| **Invested** | We talk about the client's success, not our services. |
| **Electric** | There's energy here. The name is Charged — the copy should feel it. |

---

## Tone by Section

**Hero / Headline:** Bold, declarative, one-line truth. Makes you feel something.
> ✅ "We build the brand your business deserves."
> ❌ "We provide comprehensive branding solutions for businesses of all sizes."

**Services:** Clear and benefit-led. What it does for them, not what it is.
> ✅ "Your website is your digital foundation. We build it to turn visitors into leads."
> ❌ "We offer custom website design services."

**About / Story:** Honest and human. Charlie's voice. First person where appropriate.
> ✅ "Charlie started Charged with a belief most agencies don't act on."
> ❌ "Our team of experienced professionals is dedicated to excellence."

**CTA buttons:** Short, active, charged energy. All caps where appropriate.
> ✅ "LET'S TALK" / "START A PROJECT" / "BOOK A CALL"
> ❌ "Click here to get in touch with our team"

**FAQ:** Conversational but precise. Answers the real question, not a sanitised version.

**Blog:** Educational and opinionated. We have a POV. We share it plainly.

**Retainer / Partnership copy:** Relationship-first. Long-term partner framing, not "monthly package" sales speak.

---

## Words We Use

- Build, not "create" or "develop"
- Sharp, not "professional" or "high-quality"
- Charged, electric, fired up (brand energy words — use sparingly)
- Partner, not "vendor" or "provider"
- Grow, not "scale" (unless context is right)
- Digital presence, not "online footprint"

## Words We Avoid

- "Solutions" — vague and corporate
- "Leverage" — consultant speak
- "Passionate about" — cliché
- "World-class" — overused
- "Seamless" — meaningless
- "Innovative" — everyone says it
- Excessive exclamation marks!!!

---

## Copy Principles

1. **Lead with benefit, not feature.** What does the client get, not what do we do.
2. **One idea per sentence.** If you can split it, split it.
3. **Read it aloud.** If it sounds weird, rewrite it.
4. **Punch the headline.** The first thing they read is the most important thing.
5. **End with action.** Every section should move the reader somewhere.
6. **Websites first.** The lead offer is a website. Software/apps second. Don't bury the primary service.
